## 1) The ARIA Loop — viral conversion funnel

**Thesis:** ARIA's own capability *is* the marketing. We use ARIA to produce the content that sells ARIA. The loop compounds because every deliverable is a live demo.

```
          ┌──────────────────────────────────────────────┐
          │  DAILY (ARIA runs it, human approves)        │

          │  2. Pick the 1 spiky take of the day          │

          │  4. Human 30-sec approve → publish            │
          └───────────────┬──────────────────────────────┘
                          ▼
   TikTok / Reels / Shorts  ── hook shows ARIA working ──►  profile link
                          ▼
   Landing (/) "The AI that executes" ──►  Start free (/login)
                          ▼
   Free plan: first mission delivers a real asset in minutes
                          ▼
   Daily-limit wall  ──►  Upgrade to Pro ($29)  ──►  MRR
                          ▼
   New Pro user's outputs become tomorrow's content → loop repeats
```

### Daily automation (what ARIA does)
- **Source scrape:** trending AI topics from X + Instagram (via connectors / web research).
- **Angle selection:** rank by *controversy + usefulness*; pick one.
- **Produce:** ARIA writes the 30s script, generates the b-roll image(s), and a voiceover; assembles a vertical draft.
- **Human gate:** you approve/tweak in ~30 seconds, then publish. (Keep a human in the loop — it's honest and it protects brand voice.)

## 2) LinkedIn B2B outreach — 3-message sequence

**ICP:** (a) **Founders of marketing/creative agencies** (5–30 people), (b) **Product Managers** at content-heavy SaaS. **Angle:** consultant-to-peer, ROI-first, zero hype. Selling **Pro ($29)** for individuals and **Business ($99)** for teams (5 seats/brands).

> Value framing: an agency producing daily social + blog content spends real hours on research → drafting → image/video → scheduling. Offloading the *production* layer to ARIA can free a meaningful chunk of that time. **State it as an estimate**, e.g. *"teams tell us this class of work eats 30–40 hrs/mo"* only if true; otherwise: *"if your team spends ~X hrs/mo on production, ARIA targets exactly that layer."*

> Hi {First} — I follow {Agency}'s work (the {specific piece} was sharp). I'm building ARIA, an AI that doesn't just draft but *executes* content end-to-end — research → copy → image/video/voice → publish — with a full audit log. Not selling anything here; I just talk to agency founders about where the production hours actually go. Would love to connect.

> Thanks for connecting, {First}. Quick one: on your team, who owns turning a *topic* into a finished, on-brand post — and roughly how many hours a week does that eat?
>
> The reason I ask: ARIA is built for exactly that layer. You give it a goal ("research X, make a reel + LinkedIn post, schedule it"), it does the research, writes it, generates the media, and drops the deliverable with a step-by-step trail you can review. Teams that produce daily content are the ones who feel it most.

> If it's useful, I'll send a 60-sec screen recording of it running a real brief — no call needed.

### Message 3 — Offer / CTA (3–4 days later)
> {First}, here's the 60-sec run I mentioned: [link]. That's one sentence in → finished asset out, unedited.
>
> If it looks worth 20 minutes of your team's time this week: **Pro is $29/mo** (one operator, unlimited missions) and **Business is $99/mo** (up to 5 brand workspaces + seats, scheduled autonomy, analytics). Both start free — no card — so your team can run a real brief today and judge it on the output, not my pitch.

**Cadence & guardrails:** ≤ 20 new connects/day/account (avoid LinkedIn limits & spam flags). Personalize line 1 *every time* (reference a real post). If no reply after M3, **stop** — one soft breakup message max, then move on. Track: accept-rate → reply-rate → free-signup → paid.

### Launch strategy (step by step)

**T-14 → T-2 days (prep the runway):**
1. Build a **"launch list"** — everyone who'll support: past connections, communities, LinkedIn network, indie-hacker/AI Discords/Slacks, X followers. Aim 100–300 people who *opt in* to being pinged.
2. Recruit a **Hunter** with reach (optional; self-hunting is fine now). DM 5–10 candidates.
3. Prepare assets: 3–5 clean screenshots (dashboard, live logs, a finished deliverable), a 30–45s demo GIF/video of a real mission, the gallery thumbnail.
4. Warm up: engage genuinely on PH for 2 weeks (upvote, comment) so your account isn't cold.
5. Line up **3–5 authentic testers** who used ARIA and will leave *real* comments about *their* experience. (No fake reviews — PH detects and penalizes; and it's against our honesty rule.)

**T-1 day:**
6. Schedule the launch for **12:01 AM PT** (PH resets on Pacific midnight → full 24h on the board).
7. Draft the maker's comment + 3 pre-written (but honest) answers to likely questions (pricing, data/privacy, how the autonomy works).
8. Message your launch list: *"We go live at 12:01 AM PT — I'll send the direct link the moment it's up. A comment matters more than an upvote."*

**Launch day:**
9. **00:01 PT** — publish. Post the maker's comment immediately.
10. Send the **direct link** to your list in waves (not all at once — steady drip beats a spike). Ask for **comments + questions**, not just upvotes (PH weights engagement and genuine discussion).
11. **Deliver on the promise:** actually run people's briefs live in the comments and paste the result. This is the loop again — the launch itself becomes proof.
12. Cross-post the launch to X, LinkedIn, relevant subreddits/Discords (where allowed) — link the PH page, not the site.
13. Reply to **every** comment within minutes, all day. Momentum = ranking.
14. Mid-day dip is normal — send a second wave, share a milestone honestly ("we're #4, would love your take").

**T+1 → T+7:**
15. Thank supporters publicly; DM engaged commenters an extended free trial.
16. Turn the best live-brief results into the daily reel content (loop).
17. Add a "🏆 on Product Hunt" badge to the landing **only if** we actually place — never fake it.
