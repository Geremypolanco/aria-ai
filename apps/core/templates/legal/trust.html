Trust Center

How ARIA handles your security, where we stand on compliance, and the boundaries ARIA operates within. We would rather tell you exactly where we are than imply a status we have not earned.

Last updated: July 24, 2026

Security practices in place today

  • All traffic is served over HTTPS, with HSTS enforced across the domain.
  • Baseline security headers on every response (content-type, framing, referrer, and permissions policy).
  • Sign-in is delegated to Google and GitHub OAuth — ARIA never sees or stores your password.
  • Payments are handled by Stripe; ARIA does not store card numbers.
  • Secrets live in environment configuration, never committed to the codebase.
  • ARIA runs within explicit, published operating boundaries (see Responsible AI, below).

Compliance status

We are honest about this: building toward a framework is not the same as being certified against it. Certifications such as SOC 2 and ISO 27001 require an independent third-party audit, and we will only claim them once we hold them.

SOC 2 Type II In progress

We are building the controls and evidence a SOC 2 Type II audit requires. Not yet certified.

ISO/IEC 27001 Roadmap

An information-security management system aligned to ISO/IEC 27001 is on our roadmap. Not yet certified.

HIPAA Roadmap

ARIA is not a HIPAA-compliant service today and we do not sign Business Associate Agreements yet. Please do not use ARIA to store or process protected health information (PHI).

GDPR & CCPA In progress

We honor data access and deletion requests and are formalizing our data-protection practices. Email us to exercise your data rights.

EU AI Act & AI transparency In progress

ARIA discloses AI-generated content where relevant and keeps a human in control of consequential actions. We track the EU AI Act and other AI rules as they take effect.

Responsible AI — what ARIA will and will not do

These limits are built into how ARIA works, not just written here. If a request crosses one of them, ARIA declines and offers a legitimate alternative instead.

ARIA will

  • Create original content and research with cited sources.
  • Tell you when it is unsure, when something failed, or when a claim needs checking.
  • Ask before consequential or ambiguous actions instead of guessing.
  • Disclose AI-generated content where it matters and keep you in control of what ships.
  • Draft and organize documents to work alongside your own licensed professional.

ARIA will not

  • Reproduce copyrighted text, lyrics, code, or artwork, or imitate a real artist's or brand's protected work to pass it off as authentic. It creates original work, not copies.
  • Produce counterfeit logos, trademarks, or products.
  • Help with anything illegal or that facilitates a crime.
  • Deceptively impersonate a real person or organization, create misleading deepfakes or non-consensual intimate imagery, or fabricate fake reviews or disinformation.
  • Generate content that harasses, defames, incites hatred, or exploits minors.
  • Collect or expose private personal data without a lawful basis.
  • Present itself as a licensed attorney, doctor, or financial adviser, or give professional advice in place of one.
  • Spam or violate the terms of the platforms you connect.

AI transparency & your responsibility

ARIA is an AI system. Its output can be inaccurate or incomplete, and you are responsible for reviewing anything before you publish, send, or rely on it. When ARIA can take actions on your behalf — publishing, emailing, posting to connected accounts — it is designed to keep a human in the loop and to ask before consequential or ambiguous steps.
