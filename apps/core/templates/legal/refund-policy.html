Refund Policy

Last updated: July 11, 2026

Strict no-refund policy. ARIA is a digital, compute-intensive service. The moment you subscribe and use the platform, we incur immediate and non-recoverable AI compute and media-rendering costs. For this reason, all payments and subscription fees are non-refundable, except where a refund is required by applicable law.

This policy explains how billing and refunds work for ARIA, operated by SARAPH. By subscribing to a paid plan you acknowledge and accept this policy. You are asked to confirm it explicitly at checkout before any charge is made.

1. Why payments are non-refundable

Unlike physical goods, ARIA delivers value instantly: when a mission runs, we pay third-party AI providers for tokens, image/video/audio rendering, and hosting in real time. These costs are incurred immediately and cannot be reversed once compute has been consumed. Our pricing reflects this, which is why fees are charged on a non-refundable basis.

2. Scope

  • Monthly subscriptions (Pro, Business) — fees are non-refundable, including for partial billing periods, unused time, or forgotten cancellations.
  • Renewals — automatic renewal charges are non-refundable. To avoid the next charge, cancel before your renewal date.
  • Consumed usage — any compute, generations, or media already produced are non-refundable.

3. Cancelling your subscription

You can cancel at any time from your account or the upgrade menu. Cancellation stops future renewals and takes effect at the end of your current billing period — you keep full access until then. Cancelling does not retroactively refund the current or past periods.

4. Exceptions we do honor

Even under a strict policy, we will review and, where appropriate, issue a refund in these cases:

  • Duplicate or accidental charges — for example being billed twice for the same period due to a technical error.
  • Verified billing errors — a charge that does not match the plan you selected.
  • Service-side failure — a sustained, confirmed outage on our side that prevented you from using the plan you paid for.

5. Your statutory rights

Nothing in this policy limits any non-waivable rights you may have under the consumer laws of your country or region. Where the law grants you a mandatory right to a refund or a withdrawal period for digital services, that right applies regardless of the above. Note that in many jurisdictions the right of withdrawal for digital content ends once performance begins with your prior consent — which is what you agree to when you start using ARIA.

6. How to request a review

To request a refund review under Section 4 or to exercise a statutory right, email litesaraph@gmail.com from the email address on your account within a reasonable time of the charge, describing the issue and including any relevant details (such as the charge date). Approved refunds are returned to your original payment method via Stripe.

7. Changes to this policy

We may update this policy from time to time; material changes will be posted on this page with a new "last updated" date.
